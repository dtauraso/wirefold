Three times in one session (2026-06-09) David pushed back when I stopped short of a fully-scoped task and asked permission for the remainder: I skipped the Overview tab on a weak "it's an unrelated row" inference, kept resolved/changelog tracker entries instead of removing them, and tightened 14 of 15 text-wall offenders then asked whether to do the 15th. Each time the scope was already approved and the style already calibrated.

**Why:** when the approach is agreed, asking to do the last piece reads as not finishing — it forces a redundant round-trip and signals I'm hunting for a reason to do less.

**How to apply:** once scope + style are settled, complete every in-scope item — including the last, the edge case, and the "looks unrelated" one — before reporting done. Verify completeness against the original scope (the full survey hit-list, every tab, every flagged item), not a convenient subset. Still surface genuinely-open *decisions* (a real fork only the user can pick), but never carve the final in-scope piece into an optional "want me to do this too?" If tempted to skip an item because it "seems like a different category," check it rather than infer. See [[feedback_no_manufactured_shortcuts]].
