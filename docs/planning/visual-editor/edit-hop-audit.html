Where the hop count can be minimized

Of 12 hops, 7 are mechanical pass-through (2,3,4,5,6,7,10,11 — re-wrapping the same payload across a boundary). The 3 IPC crossings are structural and stay. The savings are in the re-validate / re-serialize redundancy within each process.

−1
Hops 4 + 5 → one. The host re-parses and re-validates the EditMsg against the discriminated union (handle-message.ts:25) only to forward it. The webview already produced a typed EditMsg. Collapse onDidReceiveMessage → dispatch → case "edit" into a single typed handoff; skip the re-validate on a message this process just typed.
−1
Hops 2 + 3 → one. flushNodeMove builds the entries map and then serializes — but the RAF callback, the build, and the post are three sites for one logical "emit this frame's edit." Inline to a single per-frame send (already half-done; the batch boundary is the only real seam).
−1
Hops 10 + 11 → one. Stdout read → parse → onTraceEvent closure → post → postMessage is a 4-call relay that only re-wraps a TraceEvent in an envelope. The closure at extension.ts:86 adds nothing but the wrapper; the parse at runCommand.ts:252 and the validate at the webview (main.tsx:78) double-cover the same payload — drop one validation.

Realistic floor: 12 → ~9 hops by collapsing the three double-validate / re-serialize seams above. The 3 IPC crossings (webview↔host, host↔Go-stdin, Go-stdout↔host) and the 3 real-work hops (1 raycast, 8 graph-mutate, 9 trace-emit, 12 plot) are irreducible — they're the actual model boundaries.

Caveat (per CLAUDE.md): the TS→Go send must stay fire-and-forget and the bridge surface stays two channels. These collapses are intra-process call-chain shortening, not new bridge messages — they don't touch the bridge contract. Don't fold any Go-side timing/position logic up into the host to "save a hop"; that's the drift the model forbids.
